- Conceptual knowledge involves understanding overarching principles or [[Concept|concepts]] that govern a domain or a problem. 
- It’s about grasping the underlying framework or system that organises individual pieces of knowledge. 
- This definition includes 
	1. knowing information classification and categorisation,
	2. knowing principles and generalisations, and 
	3. knowing theories, models, and structures.  
- Basically, conceptual knowledge is knowing that facts can be organised in `meaningful ways`
