#insight 

- These books are designed to be read and used in `any order`, allowing readers to skip around and focus on what interests them.
- Traditional `background information` is included for those who want it.
- In planning area divisions, we also consider `landmarks` that will help people orient themselves when switching from one area map to the next.
- Our books use `colour` to categorise information
- Deciding how to divide a city into manageable pieces is crucial.
- Our guides offer a `unique format and way to access information`, focusing on the fabric of the city—how its parts are woven together. Unlike other guides that separate restaurants, hotels, and other attractions, our guides reflect the real, complex mix of city life.
- We `don't have the inside track` on information. But we provide a unique format and a way to gain access to it.
- The organisation of the books mirrors the `real-life arrangement` of a city, where you might find a restaurant next to a bookstore or a park next to a museum.
- My guidebooks aim to facilitate learning by providing readers with a `flexible` and `interactive` way to explore information. They offer a `detailed and realistic depiction` of an environment, allowing readers to engage with the content as if they were experiencing it in real life.
