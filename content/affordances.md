> A property in which the physical characteristics of an object or environment Influence its function.

- An affordance is a **relationship** between the **properties of an object** and the **capabilities of the agent** that determine just how the object could possibly be used.

When the affordance of an object `corresponds to it intended function`, the design will perform more efficiently.

Examples:
- Round wheels for rolling. Stairs are for climbing.
- On the door, plate is to push and handle is to pull.
- With opposing male and female surfaces and featureless sides, Legos naturally afford plugging into one another.
