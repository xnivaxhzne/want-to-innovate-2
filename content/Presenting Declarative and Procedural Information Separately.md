[[Declarative Knowledge - able to state and declare - facts and concepts - what is|Declarative information]] is defined as related to reasoning about the cause of the problem and finding a solution, whereas [[Procedural knowledge -  techniques and methods - how to - practice|procedural information]] is related to manipulating the environment.

Just-in-time strategy: Where intrinsic cognitive load is managed by initially not including the procedural aspects of the task. - [[Progressive Disclosure]]
