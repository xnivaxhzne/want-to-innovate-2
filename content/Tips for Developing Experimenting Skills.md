1. Tip #1: `Cross physical borders`:
	-  Work to cross one border at least once every month.
2. Tip #2: `Cross intellectual borders`:
	- read irrelvant publications from diff topics or from diff countries
3. Tip #3: `Develop a new skill`: 
	- [[Taking the opportunity to learn new skills in different arenas can boost your innovation capability|learn new skill]] or try out diff physical activities 
	- Job shadowing
4. Tip #4: [[Disassemble a product - taking things apart either physically or intellectually]]
5. Tip #5: `Build prototypes`:
	- Identify something that you would like to improve
	- What would it look like if you changed it?
	- Build a prototype of your new, improved invention from random materials in your house or office, or go on a shopping spree to obtain odd things that might work well in the prototype
	- If you are adventurous, buy a 3D printer.
6. Tip #6: `Regularly pilot new ideas`:
	- Engage in frequent pilot tests(small scale experiments) to try out new ideas and to see what you learn from doing something differently than you’ve done before.
	- You, too, can become an experimenter when you embrace learning through trial and error, but you must have the [[Innovators love mistakes and learn quickly from them|courage to fail and learn from your failures]]
7. Tip #7: `Go trend spotting`:
	 - Actively seek to identify emerging trends by reading books, articles, magazines, Web links, blogs, and other sources that specifically focus on identifying new trends.
	 - Then think about how these trends might lead to an interesting experiment with regard to a new product or service. 
	 - Figure out a way to creatively conduct that experiment
