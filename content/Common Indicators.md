1. Satisfaction: Are customers happy with what you’re delivering against your promises?
2. Kudos: How often do people praise you for your efforts or contributions?
3. Profit: How much was left over after expenses?
4. Value: What would someone pay for it?
5. Loyalty: How likely are your users to return?
6. Traffic: How many people used, visited, or saw what you made?
7. Conversion: What percentage of people acted the way you hoped they would?
8. Spread: How fast is word getting around about what you’re doing?
9. Perception: What do people believe about what you’re making or trying to achieve?
10. Competition: Who has similar intents to yours?
11. Complaints: How many users are reaching out about an aspect of your product or service?
12. Backlash: What negative commentary do you receive or expect?
13. Expenses: How much did you spend?
14. Debt: How much do you owe?
15. Lost time: How many minutes, hours, or days did you spend unnecessarily?
16. Drop-off: How many people leave without taking the action you hoped they would?
17. Waste: How much do you discard, measured in materials and time?
18. Murk: What alternative truths or opinions exist about what you’re making or trying to achieve?
