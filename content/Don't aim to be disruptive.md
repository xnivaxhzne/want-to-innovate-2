


For most of the people, the idea of being disruptive involves making a leap to create something that people haven't seen before. 
If they succeed at that, they actually do become disruptive. But in a bad way. Because they are asking everyone to "unlearn that and start learning this".

![[leaps.jpeg]]

---
In the book Iconic advantage, the authors observed about how the most successful long term innovators go about introducing new ideas.
Innovation tends to be an ever-present part of their process. They regularly update their products, keeping them fresh and focusing on enhancing the essence of their brand.

> The secret of constant innovation is to retain familiarity
